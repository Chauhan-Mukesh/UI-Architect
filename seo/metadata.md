# SEO — Metadata

## Purpose
Metadata tells search engines and social platforms what the page is. Get it right; do not stuff.

## Required
- `<title>` — unique per page, under 60 chars where possible, includes the primary intent.
- `<meta name="description">` — 140–160 chars, action-focused sentence.
- `<link rel="canonical">` — canonical URL for duplicate content.
- `<meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1">`.
- `<html lang>` — page language.

## Open Graph
- `og:title`, `og:description`, `og:url`, `og:image`, `og:type`.
- `og:image` at least 1200×630, under 5 MB.

## Twitter
- `twitter:card="summary_large_image"`, `twitter:title`, `twitter:description`, `twitter:image`.

## Rules
- Every page has a unique title and description.
- Match `<title>` intent to the page's primary H1.
- Do not stuff keywords.

## Anti-patterns
- Missing description.
- Site-wide identical title.
- `og:image` that doesn't exist or is enormous.
