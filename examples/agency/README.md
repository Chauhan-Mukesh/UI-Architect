# Example — Agency

## Prompt
"Website for a 10-person brand strategy agency."

## Research
- Audience: marketing leads at Series B–D companies rebranding or naming.
- Journey: they arrive after a referral; they need proof of taste and range.
- Journey risks: templated feel, missing case studies, "we do everything" positioning.

## Design direction
- Tone: distinctive, confident, editorial.
- Layout: `layouts/landing/variants/02` (story-driven) with editorial rhythm.
- Type: `editorial` voice.
- Colors: paper + ink + one confident accent (chosen to match the agency's actual taste).
- Motion: signature; deliberate hover states on selected work.

## Output plan
- Editorial hero: statement of position, not a value bullet.
- Selected work grid (6 pieces, mix of case studies).
- Capability areas written as narrative, not icon grid.
- Team / culture with real photos.
- Recognition (press, clients) if it supports positioning.
- Contact form with qualifying fields (budget, scope).

## Review notes
- Nothing on the page looks like a generic SaaS landing.
- Each case study preview earns full attention.
- No stock imagery.
- Motion feels intentional, not templated.
