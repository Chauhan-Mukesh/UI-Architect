# Psychology

## Objective
Use behavioral principles to improve trust, comprehension, and action without manipulation.

## Applied principles
- **Hick's law**: reduce simultaneous choices in critical moments.
- **Fitts's law**: make key interactive targets large and proximate.
- **Social proof**: place credible proof near decision points.
- **Loss aversion**: frame risks of inaction when appropriate.
- **Peak-end rule**: design memorable completion moments.

## Implementation guidance
- Prioritize one dominant CTA per section.
- Pair claims with evidence (metrics, testimonials, credentials).
- Sequence information from confidence-building to commitment.
- Use microcopy to reduce anxiety in forms and checkout flows.

## Guardrails
- Never create dark patterns.
- Never hide pricing/constraints behind deceptive UI.
- Preserve user agency with clear options and exits.
