# Portfolio — Best Practices

## UX priorities
- Design for hiring managers, prospective clients, or peers — decide which and prioritize.
- Show, then tell. Work is the primary evidence; copy is supporting.
- Make it obvious what the person does and what they want next.

## Content strategy
- Curate ruthlessly. 3–6 strong pieces beat 15 mediocre ones.
- Every piece needs context: role, problem, outcome, timeframe.
- Personal voice matters — a portfolio without a point of view reads as generic.

## Interaction guidance
- Rich hover states on work thumbnails; obvious click affordance.
- One direct way to contact — email or form, not both competing.
- Keep navigation minimal; a portfolio is not a corporate site.
