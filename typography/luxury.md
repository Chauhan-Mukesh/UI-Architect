# Typography — Luxury

## Purpose
Typographic voice: **refined, restrained, distinctive**.

## Pairing
- Display: Modern serif (Didone) or a distinctive serif with sharp contrast.
- Body: Elegant humanist sans or a comfortable serif.

## Rules
- Generous letter-spacing on small caps and all-caps labels
- Whitespace does the heavy lifting
- Small caps for section labels
- Body copy stays at 60–75ch measure.
- Use tabular numerals for tables and data.
- Fallback stack matches metrics of the primary.

## Anti-patterns
- Bold, aggressive display
- Cramped rhythm
- All-caps body copy.
- Ornamental script for anything beyond a headline.

## When to choose this voice
- Match against the tone adjectives in the design direction card.
- Cross-check with the industry recommendation before committing.
