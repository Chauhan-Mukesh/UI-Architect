# Typography — Font Pairings

## Purpose
Two families max, chosen for contrast and role. One-family stacks are also acceptable when the family is versatile.

## Pairing patterns
- Serif display + sans body — editorial, luxury, medical.
- Sans display + sans body (contrast in weight) — SaaS, agencies, startups.
- Sans body + monospace code — developer-facing.
- Single-family variable font — minimalism, systems where consistency > contrast.

## Rules
- Check both faces for weight coverage (regular, semibold at minimum).
- Match x-height and rhythm when pairing across families.
- Fallback stack matches the primary in metrics (`Inter, "system-ui", sans-serif`).
- License first; then choose.

## Anti-patterns
- Three or more families.
- Pairing two similarly proportioned sans faces — creates visual confusion.
- Script fonts for anything longer than a headline.
