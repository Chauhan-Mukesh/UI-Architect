# Colors — Industry

## Purpose
Every vertical has a color language visitors expect. Follow it, subvert it deliberately, or explain the subversion.

## Vertical defaults (starting points, not laws)
- **SaaS / developer tools** — blue-purple neutrals; strong accent; supports dark mode.
- **Finance / insurance** — deep blues and greens; warm neutrals; conservative accents.
- **Medical / healthcare** — calm blues, mint greens, clinical whites.
- **Education** — approachable primaries; warm neutrals; safe for varied audiences.
- **Government** — muted, high-contrast, plain; often bound to jurisdiction guidelines.
- **Legal** — deep neutrals (navy, forest, charcoal); restrained accent.
- **Real estate** — warm neutrals; property imagery carries the color.
- **Retail** — brand-driven; category patterns vary widely.
- **Restaurant / hospitality** — warm neutrals and food-flattering tones.
- **Agency / portfolio** — editorial neutrals with a single confident accent.
- **AI / ML** — neutral-forward; the "AI purple gradient" cliche should be earned, not defaulted to.
- **Open source** — developer-friendly neutrals + brand accent; parity dark mode.

## Rules
- Cross-check the chosen palette against the vertical's `industries/<v>/recommended-colors.md`.
- Do not chase competitors' palettes; follow the vertical's conventions where trust matters.
- When subverting convention, do so with clear intent and matching copy.

## Anti-patterns
- Copying a specific brand's palette (buyers who recognize the reference discount the work).
- Palettes with no relationship to the vertical or tone.
- Neon accents in medical, legal, or government contexts.
