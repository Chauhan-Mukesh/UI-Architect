# Colors — Psychology

## Purpose
Colors carry meaning by convention and context. Use those meanings deliberately.

## General associations (broad, cross-cultural, not absolute)
- **Blue** — trust, calm, technology, finance, healthcare. Overused in enterprise.
- **Green** — growth, health, money, safety. Environmental and financial cues.
- **Red** — urgency, energy, warning, appetite. Use restraint for CTAs.
- **Orange / amber** — warmth, energy, warning. Friendly without being aggressive.
- **Yellow** — attention, caution, optimism. Small doses only for large surfaces.
- **Purple** — creativity, premium, imagination. Overused in AI/creative branding.
- **Pink** — youth, warmth, approachability. Not gender-locked.
- **Black** — luxury, formality, editorial. Never absolute `#000` for body backgrounds.
- **White** — clarity, minimalism, medical. Off-white feels warmer.
- **Neutrals (gray/beige)** — restraint, professionalism. The workhorses.

## Rules
- Tie color choice to the tone adjectives from the design direction card.
- Cultural context varies (white = purity in the West, mourning in parts of Asia). Consider audience.
- Never use color alone to convey meaning.

## Anti-patterns
- Trendy palettes without tone justification.
- Rainbow gradients on serious topics.
- Semantic color reuse (red for both errors and destructive actions makes them competitive).
