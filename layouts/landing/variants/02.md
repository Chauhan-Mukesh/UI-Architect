# Landing Variant 02 — Story-Driven

## Structure
1. Hero framing the tension or moment
2. Context: why now, why this
3. Character or user in the story (persona or user testimonial as narrative)
4. Turning point / how the offering changes it
5. Evidence beat (data, quote, or artifact)
6. Path forward (what happens after conversion)
7. Final CTA with a specific next step (not "learn more")

## Use when
- Offering requires framing (nonprofits, launches, category-defining products, editorial pieces).
- Audience needs to feel the problem before they'll accept the solution.

## Grid & spacing
- Editorial rhythm: alternating full-bleed imagery and constrained-width copy.
- Longer copy sections in a narrower measure (60–70ch).

## Breakpoints
- Mobile: preserve pacing with vertical whitespace, not by breaking copy into columns.
- Desktop: allow editorial asymmetry — text offset, imagery bleeding.

## Accessibility
- Narrative imagery must have meaningful alt describing what the reader would otherwise miss.
- Pull quotes marked up as `<blockquote>` with attribution in `<cite>`.

## Anti-patterns
- Overwriting emotional weight into every section (fatigue).
- Losing the specific action at the end because the story took over.
