# Footer Layouts

## Purpose
Close the page: navigation for information seekers, legal and contact for trust, brand for identity.

## When to use which variant
- **01 Compact** — small sites and portfolios.
- **02 Structured multi-column** — SaaS, marketing sites, publications.
- **03 Editorial statement** — brand-forward sites where the footer is a coda, not a sitemap.

## Universal rules
- Include address (if applicable), contact link, primary legal links (privacy, terms), and copyright.
- Do not stuff every internal link — group thoughtfully.
- Locale/language switcher lives here if not in the header.

## Anti-patterns
- 50-link mega-footer with no hierarchy.
- Newsletter modal launching from the footer on scroll.
- Missing basics (contact, address, legal).

## Accessibility
- Footer is `<footer>` (only one per page).
- Legal links are always in tab order.
- Social icons paired with accessible names.

## Responsive
- Mobile: sections stack; groups fold if very dense.
- Desktop: columns.
