# Footer Variant 03 — Editorial Statement

## Structure
- Large statement of position, mission, or manifesto.
- Minimal supporting links (contact, legal).
- Brand mark and social icons.

## Use when
- Agencies, publications, or brand-forward products where the footer is a signature moment.

## Grid & spacing
- Statement occupies most of the footer height.
- Utility links live in a subtle bottom bar.

## Breakpoints
- Mobile: statement wraps naturally, links stack.
- Desktop: statement dominant, links compact.

## Accessibility
- Statement is a heading if it functions as such, otherwise `<p>` with clear visual weight.
- Legal links must still be reachable and pass contrast, even in editorial styling.

## Anti-patterns
- Statement that hides the address or contact behind aesthetics.
- Illegible tint-on-tint typography for legal links.
